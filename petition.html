To: Members of Congress

Last week, on July 13, millions of Americans made their support for the FCC's Open Internet Rules clear. Just one day later, FCC Chairman Ajit Pai made it equally clear that those voices would be ignored. When reporter Lynn Stanton asked Pai, "Shouldn't the number of consumers who feel they are detrimentally affected be a factor in a cost-benefit analysis of what you do?", Pai responded, "As I said previously, the raw number [of comments] is not as important as the substantive comments that are in the record." This is unacceptable. The FCC leadership should not ignore the wishes of the American people on this crucial issue. Though the FCC comment period is now closed, I ask my members of Congress to continue to publicly declare their support for the Open Internet Rules in light of Chairman Pai's recent statement. Please emphasize that Pai's statement is undemocratic and that the FCC leadership must integrate the responses made on the Day of Action into their decision-making process. Thank you!
